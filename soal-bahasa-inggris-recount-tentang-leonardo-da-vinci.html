Tentu banyak orang yang tahu siapa sang maestro pelukis Monalisa. Pelukis kenamaan asal Italia. Ya,dia adalah Leonardo Davinci. Leonardo Davinci (15 April1452 -  2 Mei 1519) adalah seorang pelukis asal desa Vinci, Provinsi Florence, Italia. Mulanya Davinci kecil hanya tinggal dengan Ibu dan kakeknya (orangtua ibu) ibunya bernama Caterina. Davinci terpaksa berpisah dengan ayahnya karena keluarga sang ayah tidak menginginkan putranya yang berdarah bangsawan menikah dengan Caterina seorang gadis desa. Bakat ingin tahu dan seni rupa Davinci memang sudah tampak sejak kecil. Bermodal kertas yang sering diberikan pastor kepadanya, banyak sketsa gambar yang dia hasilkan. Ibu dan kakeknya pun begitu kagum. Davinci sejak kecil bertangan kidal. Jadi mulai kecil dia melukis dan beraktivitas dengan tangan kiri. Memasuki usia sekolah Davinci diminta untuk ikut oleh ayah kandungnya. Melalui pastor yang kerap memberinya kertas gambar (ternyata kertas yang kerap diberikan adalah pemberian dari kakek dari ayah Da Vinci), Da vinci akhirnya mau tinggal bersama ayah dan ibu tirinya yang tidak dikarunai putra. Alasan ibu kandung Da Vinci rela melepasnya adalah demi pendidikan dan masa depan Da Vinci kelak karena kakek dan ibunya hanyalah seorang petani desa. Tinggal bersama Ser Piero Da Vinci (sang ayah) dan ibu tirinya Da vinci muda mulai menapaki jenjang keseniannya. Karya pertama yang diserahkan kepada ayahnya adalahs sebuah patung kepala medusa. Melihat kejeniusan itu akhir Ser Piero menyekolahkan Da vinci ke Florence (kota pusat seni) kepada gurunya Andrea del Verrocchio Da Vinci belajar melukis. Verrocchio pun kagum dengan karya Da Vinci yang begitu mendetail dan bisa mempresentatifkan objek sebaik mungkin. 14. penyebab kematian leonardo da vinci?


Beliau meninggal karena penyakit yang beliau deritaTidak ada catatan sejarah yang menyebut penyebab pasti kematian Leonardo Da Vinci, namun beberapa sejarawan dan juga ahli kesehatan percaya bahwa kematian Leorando Da Vinci berhubungan dengan stroke yang ia derita dua tahun sebelum ia meninggal.

Stroke ini membuat tangan kanan Da Vinci lumpuh namun ia tetap melanjutkan aktifitas melukisnya. Kelumpuhan Da Vinci ini diceritakan dalam The diary of Louis d'Aragona's journey yang ditulis oleh seseorang bernama Antonio de Beatis. Lalu dalam buku Giorgio Vasari tahun 1550 berjudull Lives of the Artists disebutkan bahwa Da Vinci seorang yang sedang sakit dan bahkan tak mampu berdiri tanpa dipapah orang lain sebagai akibat dari kelumpuhan yang ia derita.  

Stroke yang dialami Da Vinci semakin parah pada bulan bulan menjelang kematian. Menurut ahli medis hampir semua orang yang mengalami stroke akan mengalami stroke susulan kurang lebih 90 hari dari datangnya stroke pertama. Diperkirakan penyebab Da Vinci meninggal adalah serangan stroke kedua yang memperburuk kondisi kesehatannya.

Leonardo da Vinci Biography

Leonardo da Vinci : Early Life and Training

Leonardo da Vinci (1452-1519) was born in Anchiano, Tuscany (now Italy), close to the town of Vinci that provided the surname we associate with him today. In his own time he was known just as Leonardo or as “Il Florentine,” since he lived near Florence—and was famed as an artist, inventor and thinker. Da Vinci’s parents weren’t married, and his mother, Caterina, a peasant, wed another man while da Vinci was very young and began a new family. Beginning around age 5, he lived on the estate in Vinci that belonged to the family of his father, Ser Peiro, an attorney and notary. Da Vinci’s uncle, who had a particular appreciation for nature that da Vinci grew to share, also helped raise him.

Leonardo da Vinci : Early Career

Da Vinci received no formal education beyond basic reading, writing and math, but his father appreciated his artistic talent and apprenticed him at around age 15 to the noted sculptor and painter Andrea del Verrocchio, of Florence. For about a decade, da Vinci refined his painting and sculpting techniques and trained in mechanical arts. When he was 20, in 1472, the painters’ guild of Florence offered da Vinci membership, but he remained with Verrocchio until he became an independent master in 1478. Around 1482, he began to paint his first commissioned work, The Adoration of the Magi, for Florence’s San Donato, a Scopeto monastery.

Leonardo da Vinci : 'The Last Supper' and 'Mona Lisa'

Although relatively few of da Vinci’s paintings and sculptures survive—in part because his total output was quite small—two of his extant works are among the world’s most well-known and admired paintings. The first is da Vinci’s “The Last Supper,” painted during his time in Milan, from about 1495 to 1498. A tempera and oil mural on plaster, “The Last Supper” was created for the refectory of the city’s Monastery of Santa Maria delle Grazie. When Milan was invaded by the French in 1499 and the Sforza family fled, da Vinci escaped as well, possibly first to Venice and then to Florence. There, he painted a series of portraits that included “La Gioconda,” a 21-by-31-inch work that’s best known today as “Mona Lisa.” Painted between approximately 1503 and 1506, the woman depicted—especially because of her mysterious slight smile—has been the subject of speculation for centuries.

Leonardo da Vinci : Later Years

Da Vinci left Italy for good in 1516, when French ruler Francis I generously offered him the title of “Premier Painter and Engineer and Architect to the King,” which afforded him the opportunity to paint and draw at his leisure while living in a country manor house, the Château of Cloux, near Amboise in France. Da Vinci died at Cloux (now Clos-Lucé) in 1519 at age 67. He was buried nearby in the palace church of Saint-Florentin.

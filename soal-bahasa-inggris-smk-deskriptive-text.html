15. deskriptive teks tentang pemadam kebakaran dalam bahasa inggris​


A fire extinguisher is a vital firefighting tool designed to control and suppress fires in emergency situations. It is typically a cylindrical container filled with a fire-extinguishing substance under pressure. Fire extinguishers come in various types, each designed to combat specific classes of fires, such as Class A (ordinary combustibles like wood and paper), Class B (flammable liquids), and Class C (electrical fires).

The standard fire extinguisher operates using a simple mechanism. When the safety pin is pulled and the nozzle is aimed at the base of the fire, pressing the handle releases the extinguishing agent, such as water, foam, dry chemical powder, or carbon dioxide (CO2), onto the flames. This substance either cools down the fire, suppresses its oxygen, or interrupts the chemical reaction necessary for combustion.

Fire extinguishers are commonly found in various public places, homes, offices, and industrial facilities to provide immediate fire-fighting capabilities in the event of an emergency. Proper training on how to use a fire extinguisher is crucial to ensure its effectiveness and enhance safety during fire-related incidents. Regular maintenance and inspections are also necessary to ensure that the extinguisher remains operational when needed.

Jawaban:

Berikut adalah sebuah deskriptif teks tentang pemadam kebakaran dalam bahasa Inggris:

---

**Fire Extinguisher: Your First Line of Defense Against Fires**

A fire extinguisher is a vital firefighting tool designed to tackle small fires effectively. It plays a crucial role in fire safety, providing individuals with the means to control or extinguish fires in their early stages, potentially preventing them from spreading and causing further damage.

**Components:**

A typical fire extinguisher consists of several key components:

1. **Cylinder:** The cylinder is a pressure vessel that contains the extinguishing agent. It is usually made of steel or aluminum and is designed to withstand high pressure.

2. **Extinguishing Agent:** Fire extinguishers are filled with various types of extinguishing agents, such as water, foam, dry chemical powder, or carbon dioxide (CO2). The choice of agent depends on the type of fire the extinguisher is intended to combat.

3. **Nozzle or Hose:** The nozzle or hose allows the user to direct the flow of the extinguishing agent toward the fire. It helps control the discharge and prevent the user from getting too close to the flames.

4. **Pressure Gauge:** Many fire extinguishers have a pressure gauge to indicate the pressure level of the extinguishing agent. This helps ensure that the extinguisher is ready for use.

**How It Works:**

When a fire extinguisher is activated, the user typically follows the acronym "PASS" to operate it effectively:

- **P: Pull the Pin:** The first step is to pull the safety pin located on the extinguisher's handle. This action breaks the tamper seal and prepares the extinguisher for use.

- **A: Aim the Nozzle:** Aim the nozzle or hose at the base of the fire where the fuel source is. This is essential to target the source of the fire and not the flames themselves.

- **S: Squeeze the Handle:** Squeeze the handle to release the extinguishing agent. Maintain a firm grip on the handle while operating the extinguisher.

- **S: Sweep Side to Side:** Sweep the nozzle or hose from side to side in a controlled manner to cover the entire area of the fire. Continue until the fire is extinguished or the extinguishing agent is depleted.

**Importance:**

Fire extinguishers are a crucial part of fire safety plans in homes, businesses, and public places. They empower individuals to respond promptly to fires and reduce the risk of injury and property damage. Regular maintenance and training on how to use fire extinguishers are essential to ensure their effectiveness during emergencies.

In conclusion, a fire extinguisher is an indispensable tool for fire safety, allowing individuals to take immediate action when faced with a small fire. Understanding its components and how to operate it can make a significant difference in protecting lives and property in the event of a fire emergency.

Ade, Ade, Ini lah Tentang Descriptive Text

Pengertian :

Descriptive Text adalah salah satu jenis text dalam Bahasa Inggris yang menggambarkan dengan jelas sifat-sifat yang melekat pada sesuatu, baik itu manusia, hewan, tumbuhan mau pun benda mati.

Struktur Descriptive Text:

Struktur Descriptive Text (generic structure) adalah :

1. Identification (identifikasi) adalah pendahuluan , berupa gambaran umum tentang suatu topik.

2.Description (deskripsi) adalah berisi ciri-ciri khusus yang dimiliki benda, tempat, atau orang yang dideskripsikan.

Ciri-ciri Descriptive Text :

- Menggunakan simple present tense

- Menggunakan attribute verb, seperti be (am, is, are)

- Hanya fokus pada satu objek tersebut.

Berikut adalah Contoh Descriptive Text :

The towering monument symbolizes the philosophy of Lingga and Yoni. Lingga resembles, rice pestle (alu) and Yoni resembles a mortar rice (lesung), two important items in Indonesian agricultural tradition.

The construction began in 1961 under the direction of President Soekarno and the monument was opened to the public in 1975. It is topped by a flame covered with gold foil. The monument and museum is opened daily from 08.00 – 15.00 every day throughout the week, except for the last Monday of the month the monument is closed.

MAKASSAR
Makassar is the provincial capital of South Sulawesi, Indonesia, and the largest city on Sulawesi Island. From 1971 to 1999, the city was named Ujung Pandang, after a pre-colonial fort in the city, and the two names are often used interchangeably. The port city is located at 5°8′S 119°25′E, on the southwest coast of the island of Sulawesi, facing the Makassar strait. Its area is 175.77 km2 and has population of around 1.4 million.

Makassar is home to several prominent landmarks including the 16th century Dutch fort Fort Rotterdam, Trans Studio Makassar the third largest indoor theme park in the world and the Karebosi Link the first underground shopping center in Indonesia.

Makassar has several famous traditional foods. The most famous is Coto Makassar. It is a stew made from the mixture of nuts and spices with beef parts which include beef brain, tongue and intestine. Konro rib dish is also popular traditional food in Makassar. Both Coto Makassar and Konro are usually eaten with Burasa, glutinous rice with coconut milk and sauted coconut granule.

In addition, Makassar is the home of pisang epe, or pressed bananas. These are bananas which are pressed, grilled, and covered with palm sugar sauce and sometimes eaten with Durian. Many street vendors sell pisang epe, especially around the area of Losari beach.

The Houses of the Toraja
The ethnic groups in the mountain regions of southwest and central Sulawesi (Celebes) are known by the name of Toraja, which has come to mean “those who live upstream” or “those who live in the mountains”. Their name is in fact derived from the word Raja, which in Sanskrit means “king”. The society is hierarchically structured: the noblemen are called rengnge, the ordinary people to makaka, and the slaves to kaunan; birth determines which rank a person will occupy.

The distinctive features of the traditional houses (tongkonan) of the Toraja are the “buffalo horns”, the roof design and the rich decoration on the walls. The buffalo is a symbol of status, courage, strength and fighting spirit.

Designed as a representation on the universe, the tongkonan is constructed in three parts: the upper world (the roof), the world of humans (the middle of the building), and the underworld (the space under the floor). The highly distinctive roors constructed by the Toraja given rise to various ingenious interpretations. Certainly the roof is something of deep significance for the Toraja, and even today they build “modern” (in other words houses built with cement) houses with such roofs.

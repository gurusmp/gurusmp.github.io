Cat

Cat or Felis (called in Latin), are carnivorous or meat-eaters. The word “cat” is said to represent an animal that has been domesticated. However, cats actually can be a tiger, lion, and cheetah which are a kind of wild animal.

A home cat which is quite familiar, is a mammal that has four legs, a tail, two pointy ears, and bushy hair. The feet have the sharp claws. You must be careful when holding the leg of a cat, because the claws are so powerful and can hurt both your hands and body.

Cat’s tail is used as a tool to communicate and to maintain the body balance. Two pointy cat ears have very good hearing. Cats even have a hearing performance two times than dogs and seven times better than human. The bushy hair is an attraction because some colors are striking and beautiful.

One thing that you should not forget is the cat’s moustaches. Cats have moustaches whose function is to detect preys in the dark. It is also used to know their emotions. Cat as the most popular pet in the world has a weight between 2.5 to 7 kilograms. Only a few cats that are able to weigh up to 10 kilograms. However, if fed in excess, you can see that the cat has a weight up to 23 pounds.

They can live up to 15-20 years. However, we now see that a cat only just able to live up to the age of 3-4 years. Meanwhile, they called as the sleeping animal. Cats can spend up to 16 hours every day only for sleeping. In addition to sleep, their another habit is licking their body in order to cleanse the body of impurities.

In Indonesia, we usually find some kind of cats including domestic cats, Persian, Angora, Himalayan, Maine Coon, and Bengal Cats.

22. contoh describing place wisata tangkuban parahu (memakai bahasa inggris)


   TANGKUBAN PERAHU MOUNT

Tangkuban perahu is a volcano, 25 kilometers north of Bandung direction of Lembang. Its location is in between Sagalaherang village, Sagalaherang district, Subang regency and Cikole village, Lembang district, Bandung regency. This is Bandung's most famous tourist volcano just 28 km north of the city. This volcano offers many places to see and explore. Whether we look into the huge crater or hike down into it, stroll through the forest on its slopes, or simply enjoy the splendid panoramic scenery. Mount Tangkuban Perahu is an interesting destination that everyone in the Bandung area is fond of visiting. When seen from Bandung, Mount Tangkuban Perahu has a distinctive shape, like an upside down boat. Tangkuban Perahu means, in fact, "up-turned boat" This peculiar shape has stimulated the fantasy of the Sundanese people from early times as expressed in the Legend of Sangkuriang. Geologically, Mount Tangkuban Perahu has played a significant role in the development of Parahyangan highlands. Eruptions have contributed immensely to the hills north of Bandung through lava flowing into the valleys and hardening into rock, thus forming big cliffs over which waterfalls leap. Likewise, mud flows have formed a semi-circular cone of gentle gradient , which is now a mass that blocked the valley of the ancient Citarum River near present day in Padalarang (some 18 km west of Bandung), this caused a lake to form covering the whole Bandung plain.

26. buatlah paragraf tentang animals dengan bahasa inggris


The Lion and the Mouse

One day, a Lion was sleeping in the forest. A little Mouse passed by and saw the Lion. He liked the Lion's mane and started to play with it. The Lion got up and got very angry. He caught the Mouse in his paws and roared loudly. He wanted to eat the Mouse but the Mouse squeaked. He asked the Lion not to eat him. He promised the Lion to help him someday. The Lion laughed but he let the Mouse go.

Some days later, the Lion got caught in a hunter's net. He roared and tried to break free but he couldn't. He roared loudly and asked for help. The Mouse heard the Lion's roar. He come and helped the Lion. He bit the net with his sharp teeth and the Lion was free. He thanked the Mouse for his help. From that day on, the Lion and the Mouse were good friends.

Rafael: Yeah, where did you get that?

Chris: That iguana? oh that's actually my sister's. She got it when she was on a trip to Mexico.

S: whoa, she took it from the barren desert with her own hand?

C: No, not even close. Her friend gave her that.

R: what does it eat?

C: some chicken and some ants or any ins-ects we can get.

S: I see, it does bite, right?

C: Well, technically yes, but it ain't as scary as the crocodile or komodo.

R: but can it run fast?

C: Sure it can.

S: Do you always put it in a cage?

C: well, not exactly. But it's not me who does it.

R: Does it ever come out and be on a lose?

S: Well, if it was on the lose, then it wouldn't come back just as cats.

animal origin - where does it come from? food - what does it eat? natural skill - what can it do?physical appearance - what does it look like?

13. Buatlah dialog bahasa inggris menggunakan getting one's attention, checking understanding,show appreciation, dan give opinion (untuk 2 orang)​


Jawaban:

Person A: Hey, have you seen my keys?

Person B: (Getting one's attention) Oh, excuse me, I think I saw your keys on the kitchen counter.

Person A: Great! (Checking understanding) Did you put them there?

Person B: No, I didn't. I just happened to notice them there a few minutes ago.

Person A: Thanks a lot for letting me know. (Show appreciation) I really appreciate your help.

Person B: No problem! (Give opinion) It's always good to have someone around who can spot things when they're misplaced.

21. membuat dialog bahasa inggrisdari soal berikut ini1. your friends is getting influenza2. your sister Will hold a birthday party tonight3. your brother Will have a national examination next week​


Jawaban:

(1)  Your friends is getting influenza

A: Hey, how are you feeling?

B: Not so good, I think I'm coming down with something.

A: Oh no, what is it?

B: I think it's the flu. My throat is sore and I have a fever.

A: That doesn't sound good. Have you seen a doctor?

B: No, not yet. But I've been resting and taking over-the-counter medicines for the symptoms.

A: Take care of yourself and drink plenty of fluids. Is there anything else you need?

B: Thank you for your concern. Can you help me get some groceries? It's hard for me to go out in this condition.

A: Of course! Just send me the list, and I'll get everything for you.

B: That would be a great help. Thank you so much!

A: Don't mention it. Take care and get well soon!

B: Thanks again, I really appreciate it.

(2) Your sister Will hold a birthday party tonight

Person A: Hey, did you hear that your sister is going to hold a birthday party tonight?

Person B: Oh really? That's great news! Where is she having the party?

Person A: She's hosting it at our house. Mom and dad agreed to let her use the backyard for the celebration.

Person B: That sounds like a fun location! Is there going to be any special theme or decorations?

Person A: Yes, my sister decided on a Hawaiian luau theme. So, expect to see lots of tropical decorations and maybe even some grass skirts!

Person B: Awesome! I love Hawaiian-themed parties. What time does the party start?

Person A: The guests are supposed to arrive around 7 p.m., so we should probably get everything ready beforehand.

Person B: Definitely, we don't want to keep everyone waiting. Are there any specific activities or games planned for the party?

Person A: My sister has organized some traditional Hawaiian games like Limbo and Hula Hoop contests. It should be entertaining for everyone.

Person B: Sounds like a blast! Do you need any help with setting up or preparing anything before the guests arrive?

Person A: Actually, yes! It would be great if you could help us set up some tables and chairs in the backyard. We also need assistance with hanging up decorations.

Person B: Of course! I'll be glad to lend a hand in making sure everything looks perfect for your sister's special day.

(3) Your brother Will have a national examination next week

A: Hey, I heard that your brother will have a national examination next week. How is he preparing for it?

B: Yes, you're right! He's been studying really hard. He's been attending extra classes and spending long hours revising his notes.

A: That's great to hear! Is he feeling confident about the exam?

B: Well, he is a bit nervous because this exam is quite important for his future academic pursuits. But overall, he feels well-prepared and determined to do his best.

A: I'm sure he'll do great! Does he have any specific strategies or study techniques that he's using?

B: Absolutely! He has created a study schedule to manage his time efficiently. He breaks down each subject into smaller topics and focuses on them one by one. Additionally, he practices past exam papers to get familiar with the format and types of questions.

A: That sounds like a solid plan. Is there anything we can do to support him during this crucial time?

B: Definitely! Encouragement and moral support mean a lot to him. We can remind him of how capable he is and offer help whenever needed, whether it's proofreading essays or quizzing him on different subjects.

A: That's wonderful. It shows how much you care about his success. I wish your brother all the best for his national examination!

B: Thank you so much for your kind words! I'll pass on your wishes to him.
